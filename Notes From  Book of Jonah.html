Notes From the Book of Jonah

Who wrote the book

Unknown Author

Why was it written

  1. The account of Jonah the son of Amittai
  1. To reveal the mind of God concerning Nineveh
  1. To reveal the mind of God concerning the Lost nations of the world. (e.g Nineveh)
  1. To give a historical antecedent of the salvation of the nation of Nineveh

 

What's the truth in the book

  1. God is abundant in lovingkindness chap 4:5
  1. God is super patient with man and doesn’t wish that any should perish chap 4:11
  1. Disobedience to God can cause you loads of pain (literal loss both to self and those around) chap 1:7
  1. The entire universe hears God’s voice chap 2:10
  1. God sees your heart before your face chap 3:8-10

 

Chapter 1 (Message Unto Jonah)

Verses 1 -3 describes the message to Jonah the son of Amittai to visit the city of Nineveh to do God’s bidding but somehow he was headed towards Tarshish. Tarshish here could mean so many things to some of us, Notice in verse 3 the Word specifically tells us from the presence of the Lord? If we remember vividly apostle Peter made an outcry to Jesus “Whither shall we go from you ? to whom shall we turn” I’m not sure why Jonah took that route but most certainly his understanding of how far God can see is myopic.

verses 4 and 5presents to us God sending a whirlwind into the sea where Jonah had sought comfort, just to pass a message across to Jonah, God was literarily saying Son you can run away from me but you can’t run too far (chuckles)

verse 6speaks particularly to me wither it said

what meanest thou, O sleeper? arise, call upon thy God, if so be that God will think upon us, that we perish not

in order words, sleeper in the spirit it’s time for a reawakening, call upon God for us not to perish

verse 7 speaks particularly about the occupants of the ship casting lots and it falling upon Jonah, there and then is the big question asked, will a lot cast fall on a prophet? Now follow closely he had disobeyed God blatantly in chapter 1 verse 3 and the scriptures say the sin of disobedience is like witchcraft in a broader perspective every time a man vehemently disobeys God he’s ranked like a witch you want an example? Saul, Jonah.. the periscope of the sorcerer hence could cross his radar. That was the case with Jonah folks

verses 8 to 10 revealed the co ship occupants asking Jonah what, where, and who he was. He gave a very pungent response as he answered from the angle of

  1. Origin - where he is from in this case was that he is a Hebrew
  1. Conviction - He stated categorically what he believed in ( and I fear the Lord)
  1. Gave a little explanation of his conviction ( let people have a reason for your conviction)

verses 9-14 shows how the ship occupants purposed to throw Jonah into the sea according to what Jonah said, in a clearer perspective the sea is known to consume everything that goes into it .. God understood that principle so he prepared a big fish because God knew the sea was not an accommodating ground. You see every time we’re thrown into a place of destruction a fourth man always shows up

Chapter two (Jonah’s Cry for Help)

Verses 1 to 4 of this particular chapter shows us Jonah crying to God from the belly of the fish... in other words where he called God from didn’t matter to him as long as he is heard. Look people calling upon God doesn’t have to do with specific location or timing because the scriptures outline that the eyes of the Lord go to and fro the earth and working on the behalf of those that love Him.

Verse 4b he (Jonah) summoned the courage and said unto God, I will look again unto Thy Holy temple, in other words, I’m very much aware that my help cometh from thee (psalm 121:1) so hence I turn to thee.

verses 5 and 6 something outstanding about this particular verse 5& 6 God brings us out from every depth despite where we’ve been and how it has been

verse 8: They that observe lying vanities forsake their mercy-- whatever you serve thus (lying vanities ) takes you certain steps away from God’s mercy, as much as His mercies are new every morning that doesn’t call for us to take it for granted.

verse 10 - A concrete proof that the earth is the Lord’s and the fullness thereof is this scripture, even the fish had an ear for the Lord

Then the Lord ordered the fish to spit Jonah out onto the beach

That is to say, circumstances may surround you but for sure, just a word can bring you out of such ( and He sent forth His word and delivered them from all their disease...)

 

Chapter Three (Jonah Finally Yielded and the people cried To God)

Verses1 -3spoke about the word of the Lord coming to Jonah and Jonah obeying this time around, notice in verse 4 All jonah needed to do was just speak those words. Always heaven’s agenda for us is to obey, do the Lord’s biddings and he will cause the nations to give you listening ears. God spoke Jonah finally listened and the people too listened...

Verse 7 of this particular chapter says something profound

and he caused it to be proclaimed and published through Nin-e-veh by the decree of the kings and nobles...

The king proclaimed( He said something) but much more importantly he needed to put it in writing( published), what I'm trying to point out is that as believers we’re are not just to say stuff within the context of speaking alone, let’s avail ourselves the opportunity of the world to see our publications ( This could be music, literature, craft e.t.c)

Again we know that kings make proclamations, and we’re all kings right (1 pet 2:9), Rev 1:6️⃣

Verses 8-10 reveals how the king declared a fast for everything in Nineveh, something pungent we need to learn from here quickly folks When they entreated God in a fast, the first thing God saw was their works (Turning away from their evil deeds), now God didn’t even look at their faces or clothing or bodily disfiguring by the fast rather he looked at the outworking of the heart (Their works).

“ Fasting is not complete when the works are left untended to...”

Is this the fast I have chosen: a day for a man to deny himself, to bow his head like a reed, and to spread out sackcloth and ashes? Will you call this a fast and a day acceptable to the LORD? Isaiah 58:5-7

Isn’t this the fast that I have chosen: to break the chains of wickedness, to untie the cords of the yoke, to set the oppressed free and tear off every yoke?7Isn’t it to share your bread with the hungry, to bring the poor and homeless into your home, to clothe the naked when you see him, and not to turn away from your flesh and blood?

Notice that Isaiah 58:6&7 gives us a picture of what an ideal fast is 1. Warfare 2. Giving

 

Chapter Four A conversation between God & His Servant

verses1-3 of the book shows Jonah questioning God and informing God the reason for his rebellion --(to him God is too merciful).

Verse 5shows an epic display of God’s Loving-kindness, Notice how Jonah speaks of God in verse 2 as being merciful and abundant in Loving-kindness? that is the case in this verse a practical display of abundant lovingkindness.

There’s something interesting about this entire chapter, God took on the role of a negotiator as evident in verses 10 and 11 wherein God had asked Jonah in Verse 11

And should I not pity Nineveh, that great city, in which are more than one hundred and twenty thousand persons who cannot discern between their right hand and their left- and much livestock?
